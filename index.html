Dire Straits

      Dire Straits were a British rock band formed in London in 1977. Their first hit single (Sultans of Swing) which reaching the top ten in the UK and Us charts in 1978 established the group's commercial appeal on both sides of the Atlantic. This success of the group continued with its second album "Communique" (1979). "Communique" strengthened its ties with the group's listeners, and the album sold 3 million copies worldwide. Then in 1980, Dire Straits were nominated for two Grammy Awards. At the same year David Knopfler(guitarist of group) left the group for his solo career while Dire Straits was recordig their third album "Making Movies". After two years, their fourth studio album "Love Over Gold". This album gained gold album award in US; moreover, it had stayed number one in UK for four weeks. The group has became quite around 1983-1985. By 1985, their new album "Brothers in Arms" which was sold 9 million copies was inflection point for Dire Straits. "80s best-selling album" caption was given to this album; furthermore, the group reached international status. For contributing that group gave many concerts. Then Dire Straits disbanded in 1988 but re-formed to release " On Every Street" in 1991. However this did not be succesful and the group fell into a gap again.

Group Members

  1. Mark Knopfler : Lead vocals, guitar (1977-1995)
  2. John Illsley: Bass, vocals, guitar (1977-1995)
  3. Pick Withers: Drums (1977-1982)
  4. David Knopfler : Guitar, keyboards, vocals (1977-1980)
  5. Alan Clark: Keyboards (1980-1995)
  6. Hal Lindes : Guitar (1980-1985)
  7. Terry Williams : Drums (1982-1989)
  8. Guy Fletcher : Keyboards, guitar, vocals (1984-1995)
  9. Jack Sonni : Guitar (1985-1988)
